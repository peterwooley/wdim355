WDIM355 Client-Side Scripting with Peter Wooley

Final Project Proposal, due End of Class Week 7.

Please upload your proposal to the Dropbox on eCompanion, using the following naming convention: lastnameFirstname_finalProposal.[filetype]

Abstract

You will have four (4) weeks of class to design, develop, and deploy a JavaScript-based Web Application.

You may work on the project alone, in pairs, groups of three, groups of four, groups of five, groups of six, groups of seven, or as one big group.

Requirements

  • Develop a proposal for your final project in .html, .txt or .pdf.
  • Write your proposal with these final project requirements in mind:
    • Your final project concept should be a Web Application that you want to use.
    • The Web App may be novel, mashed-up, or just better than its competition.
    • It may incorporate Object-oriented principles in its creation.
    • It should utilize jQuery for DOM interactions and general utility.
    • The user experience should be polished.
    • The design should be thought-out, purposeful, and non-arbitrary.
    • There should be no inline JavaScript.
  • Your Purposal should fulfill these requirements:
    • Show who is working on the project.
    • Explain what the final project is.
    • Define what the project does.
    • Give technical details on how the project will work.
    • Declare an estimated number of work hours for completion.
    • Argue why the project matters.

Examples

  • Do you want to play a Web-based Real-time Strategy game? There's your idea.
  • Do you pine for an online Photo Application? Build it.
  • Is an Ajax-based Chat Application up your alley? Hack away.

Grading Rubric

  • 20% Interaction Design
  • 30% Code functions properly, is written clearly, has good comments.
  • 50% Completed the scope of the project, project is of sufficient size and complexity, everything works.

Weekly Breakdown

  • Week 7: Group up, work on project concept.
  • Week 8: Present concepts to the class. Work on the hardest technical challenge your project faces.
  • Week 9: Work, work, work. Meet for Visual Design critique with Peter. Design comps must be complete.
  • Week 10: Meet for Functional Code Complete critique with Peter. Core functionality must be complete.
  • Week 11: Present the projects.
